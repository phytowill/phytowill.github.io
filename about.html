About

William P. Dwyer

Plant Biologist · PhD Candidate

Hello and thanks for reading through my page! A little about me: I was raised outside Paris, in France, where I spent my childhood collecting periodic table elements from Ebay and mixing hydrochloric acid with tin foil in my family's garage. Spoiler: it goes boom. I was first corrupted by biology during my undergrad studies in New York. After a brief excursion in the biomedical world, I fell in love with plants and their otherworldly shapes. I followed my newfound interest to (metaphorically) greener pastures in California where I trained as a plant biologist first with Seung Yon Rhee and currently with José Dinneny. My interests are at the interface of biotic and abiotic systems, specifically: how do cells/tissues/organisms perceive their environment? I use the root—an innately sensitive organ—as a system to understand how plants sense their surroundings and regulate their development as the world around them changes.

I enjoy side-quests and meandering through branches of science. My lifelong aspiration is to resolve some of nature's magic and share it through storytelling. I hope this page can be a starting point for that.
